You built an agent. Now what?

🔓

No permission model

Your agent can access everything. There's no tiered permission system, no boundary enforcement, no least-privilege defaults.

📝

No audit trail

Something goes wrong at 3am. Who approved that action? What data did the agent read? Unstructured logs won't cut it in an audit.

🚨

No kill switch

The agent starts doing something unexpected. How do you stop it? How fast? Most teams can't answer that question.

🤝

No approval workflow

Your agent sends an email, commits code, or makes a purchase — with nobody reviewing it first. One bad action and you lose trust.

📊

No compliance story

A client asks "how do you govern your AI?" and you say "we're careful." That's not an answer. You need a framework.

🏗️

Everyone reinvents it

Every team duct-tapes their own governance layer. Inconsistent, untested, and undocumented. There should be a standard.

Four trust levels. Explicit promotion.

Every agent starts at T0. Capabilities are earned, not assumed.

Tier 0

Observer

Read-only. Analyze data, surface findings. No external actions. Zero risk.

Tier 1

Drafter

Generate drafts and proposals. Every action requires human approval before execution.

Tier 2

Executor

Execute pre-approved action classes autonomously. Exceptions escalate to humans.

Tier 3

Autonomous

Full authority within scoped boundaries. Post-hoc audit. Anomaly-triggered alerts.
